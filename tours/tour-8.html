You can explore the National Museum, which displays various ancient items, historic artifacts, archaeological discoveries, and paleontological objects, such as the 3.5 million-year-old Lucy skeleton. Additionally, you can visit the impressive St. George Cathedral, which houses fascinating contemporary paintings and mosaics. The Mercato, which is the largest open market of its kind in Africa, is also worth a visit. Finally, don't miss the breathtaking panoramic view from Mount Entoto.

Bahir Dar, north-ethiopia

Bahir Dar is a charming city located on the shore of Lake Tana, Ethiopia's largest lake. Upon arrival at our hotel, we embark on a boat tour to explore Lake Tana and its 37 islands, 30 of which are home to churches and monasteries of significant cultural and historical value. During our visit, we explore the Ura Kidane Mihiret and Azwa Mariam monastery churches. Ura Kidane Mihiret is the most famous of the monasteries and houses a remarkable collection of religious icons dating back to the 16th to 18th centuries A.D. Azwa Mariam is renowned for its paintings, and we also take some time to visit the small museum.

Later in the afternoon, we head to the Blue Nile Falls, known locally as Tissisat, where the water drops 45 meters (148 feet), creating mist and rainbows. We descend to the base of the falls for a closer look and then ascend the opposite side. The entire round trip, which involves walking, takes approximately an hour. We then return to our starting point by boat, where we spend the night at the hotel.

Gonder and Simen Mountains

Gondar was the first capital city of the Ethiopian Empire, which began in 1632 with the reign of Fasilidas. In Gondar, there are a dozen castles built by various emperors over the course of 236 years.

After traveling for a distance of 3 km from Gondar, we will make a brief stop to visit the village of the Ethiopian Felasha community (also known as Black Jews). Then, we will proceed towards the Simien Mountains, which have been declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and will be our destination for the day.

Lalibela, North-ethiopia

You'll know visit the city Lalibela and the first group of churches Lalibela, contains 11 monolithic churches that were built in the 12th century and are carved out of the pink granite rock; they have been classified as a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
